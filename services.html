Services

I am pleased to offer in-home and virtual visits. To provide the best care for your family, all initial visits after your baby’s birth are conducted either in-home. I am currently observing recommended COVID-19 precautions.

Initial visits

In-home (2 hrs) $220
Multiples (> 1 baby) +$30/baby
Travel beyond service area +$1/mi

Initial visits include a feeding plan designed for you and your family; assessment of baby’s weight; a follow-up video call within 48 hrs to check-in; unlimited support via text or email for one week following the home visit; and communication with your healthcare provider as appropriate.

Follow-up visits and back-to-work consults

In-home (1 hr) $150
Virtual (1 hr) $100

Follow-up visits and back-to-work consults are scheduled as needed following an initial in-home visit. They typically include: a detailed check-in on how things are going; continued monitoring of your baby’s weight; troubleshooting if necessary; and future planning (returning to work, maintaining milk supply, pumping/feeding schedules, etc.)

Prenatal visits

Virtual (30 min) Free

Prenatal visits provide an opportunity to: get to know each other; answer your questions/concerns regarding feeding; help develop a feeding “game plan”; evaluate how to set up your home to make feeding easier; assist in choosing and learning how to use your breast pump; and more!

Complimentary virtual prenatal visits include a brief introductory video call to answer any questions/concerns you may have and to discuss working together to meet your feeding goals.

Latching package for new moms

3 visit pkg. (save 10%) $470

Maximize breastfeeding success and save 10% on a package of three visits focused on helping mom and baby:

  • Establish milk supply
  • Perfect the latch
  • Learn how to use your breast pump
  • Arrange your feeding station
  • …and more!

The timing of visits will be individualized per family. Please only schedule the first appointment at time of initial booking. After the initial appointment we will work together to schedule the following two visits.

Billing info

At the end of every visit, I will provide you with a medical bill that you can submit to your insurance company. Reimbursement is not guaranteed. I accept all major credit cards and HSA/FSA cards via Square. Gift cards are also available for purchase.

As per the State of MinnesotaCare Tax (Minnesota Statutes. Sec. 295.50), a 1.8% tax is applied to all lactation visits at the time of service.

A $50 cancellation fee will be charged to your card if your appointment is cancelled with less than 24 hours notice.

Additional time beyond the scheduled appointment duration will be billed at a rate of $100/hr.

Insurance

I’m excited to partner with The Lactation Network, a company that helps families get expenses related to lactation care covered by their insurance. They guarantee every family three 90-minute, in-home and/or virtual lactation consultations with no out of pocket cost. The Lactation Network handles all paperwork and billing directly and currently work with the following insurance companies:

  • BCBS PPO
  • Anthem BCBS PPO
  • Cigna PPO
  • United PPO

To learn more about getting your lactation care covered by your insurance visit The Lactation Network. To schedule a consultation with me, use my name as the IBCLC who referred you.

Appointments tailored for you and your baby may include:

  • Weighted feeds to assess how much milk your baby is transferring at the breast
  • Assistance with positioning, latching, manual expression, and more
  • Guidance and hands-on instruction on the efficient use of your breast pump
  • Using breastfeeding tools to optimize your breastfeeding experience (nipple shield, nipple everter, etc)
  • Feeding more than one baby (tandem feeding, feeding schedule for multiples, triple feeding, etc.)
  • Troubleshooting feeding problems specific to parent and baby dyad
  • Prescription treatment for common breastfeeding problems without a visit to your healthcare provider
  • Establishing and supporting chestfeeding
  • Evidence-based guidance on how to manage your milk supply (boosting supply, decreasing supply, weaning, storing milk, etc.)
  • Supporting your transition back to work as a breastfeeding parent and developing a plan as needed
  • Instruction on how best to bottle feed a breastfed baby
  • Hands on instruction on supplementation (cup/spoon feeding, syringe feeding, finger feeding, SNS usage, paced bottle feeding, etc.)
  • Referral to and communication with your healthcare provider when additional medical care is required
